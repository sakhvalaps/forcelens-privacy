Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 2025  ·  Version 1.0

The short version

All log analysis happens locally in your browser — nothing is sent to ForceLens servers.
Your Salesforce session cookie is used only to call Salesforce APIs on your behalf.
AI features are fully opt-in. Your own API key goes directly to your chosen provider.
No personal data, browsing history, or log content is ever sold or shared.
The optional Support portal stores only what you voluntarily submit (bug reports).
You can delete your support submissions at any time.

1. Who we are

ForceLens is a Chrome extension built for Salesforce developers. It helps you analyze Apex debug logs directly in your browser. The extension is developed and maintained by the ForceLens team and distributed through the Chrome Web Store.

Questions? Reach us at the Support portal inside the extension or via the contact details at the bottom of this page.

2. What data we collect (and what we don't)

ForceLens is designed with a local-first, privacy-first architecture. Here is a precise breakdown.

3. Why we need each browser permission

Chrome requires us to declare every permission. Here is exactly why each one is needed.

PermissionWhy it's needed
storage
unlimitedStorage		 Salesforce debug logs can reach 10–50 MB. Chrome's standard 5 MB storage quota would silently lose data on any real enterprise log. unlimitedStorage ensures the full log is preserved locally for analysis. All data stays on your device.
cookies The Salesforce REST and Tooling APIs require a Bearer token — the sid session cookie. Salesforce intentionally sets this as an HttpOnly cookie, meaning page scripts and content scripts cannot read it for security reasons. Only the extension background worker (with this permission) can read it via chrome.cookies.get(). The token is used in memory for the API call and is never stored or sent anywhere other than back to your Salesforce org. This is the same technique used by Salesforce Inspector.
tabs ForceLens opens its analysis workspace as a browser tab. This permission is required to open, reuse, or focus that tab. No tab URLs, titles, or page content are read or transmitted.
activeTab Used to detect which Salesforce page you are currently on when you trigger the extension, so the correct log context is passed to the workspace.

4. AI features — how they work

AI Pulse, Ask AI, and Flow AI Summary are fully opt-in. They are disabled by default and require you to enter your own API key from a supported provider (Anthropic Claude, OpenAI, Groq, or OpenRouter).

When you trigger an AI analysis:

✔ A structured summary of the analyzed log (method names, DML/SOQL counts, error types) is sent to your chosen provider — not the raw log text.

✔ Your API key is stored only in your browser's local storage and is sent directly to your provider. ForceLens never receives or stores it.

✔ Each provider's own privacy policy governs how they handle the data you send them.

✔ You can remove your API key and disable AI features at any time in the Configure AI settings.

5. Data storage and retention

Local data (log content, preferences, bookmarks, API keys): stored in chrome.storage.local on your device. It persists until you clear your browser extension storage, uninstall the extension, or manually clear it from the Settings view. ForceLens has no access to this data.

Support portal submissions: stored in our Supabase database. You can delete your own posts and replies at any time directly from the Support portal UI. We do not retain deleted submissions.

Salesforce session token: read into memory for the duration of a Tooling/REST API call and immediately discarded. It is never written to any storage.

6. Third-party services

Supabase — used for the optional Support portal (bug reports and feature requests). Data is stored in the EU region. See supabase.com/privacy.

AI providers (opt-in only) — Anthropic, OpenAI, Groq, OpenRouter. Each provider's privacy policy applies to data you send through their API. ForceLens does not control or have visibility into how they process data.

ForceLens does not use any analytics, telemetry, tracking pixels, or advertising SDKs.

7. Children's privacy

ForceLens is a professional developer tool intended for users aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect any information from children under 13. If you believe a child has submitted data through the Support portal, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

8. Your rights

Because ForceLens stores almost all data locally on your device, you have full control over it at all times. You can:

✔ Delete local data — clear extension storage from your browser or uninstall the extension.

✔ Delete support submissions — use the Delete button on your own posts and replies in the Support portal.

✔ Disable AI features — remove your API key from the Configure AI settings at any time.

If you have questions or requests about data held in our Support portal database, contact us at the address below.
